MATcH

BERT is not The Count: Learning to Match Mathematical Statements with Proofs

MATcH introduces a large-scale task for matching mathematical statements to their corresponding proofs, combining bilinear similarity models with local and global decoding strategies.

Weixian Waylon Li · Yftah Ziser · Maximin Coavoux · Shay B. Cohen

Task

Match each theorem-like statement with its proof

Given a collection of statements and candidate proofs, MATcH scores statement-proof pairs with a trainable bilinear model. Local decoding ranks proofs independently for each statement, while global decoding enforces one-to-one assignments.

A

Bilinear similarity

Encoded statements and proofs are compared with a learned bilinear association score.

B

Global decoding

Assignment constraints prevent many statements from selecting the same high-scoring proof.

Dataset

Mathematical statements and proofs from research articles

MATcH is extracted from arXiv articles based on the MREC corpus. It includes mixed and unmixed variants, plus conservation, partial, full, and transposition symbol replacement levels.

Mixed split

Statement-proof pairs from the same article can appear across train, dev, and test.

Unmixed split

Pairs from the same article appear in only one split, creating a harder setting.

PB subset

A probability-domain restriction of the mixed dataset.
